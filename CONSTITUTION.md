# Liberty Intelligence Labs: Constitution

**Liberty Intelligence Labs** is a student-run modular research and development unit at [Liberty College](https://libertycollege.edu.np/) institutionally backed by the CSIT Department. We operate as a project-based collective with an exclusive focus on AIoT and cybersecurity to execute the vision defined in our [Manifesto](https://github.com/libertyintelligencelabs/.github/blob/main/profile/README.md). This Constitution serves as the primary governing framework and the highest authority within the lab. All SOPs, House Styles, and member activities must align with the structures defined herein.

## Governance
The lab is directed by a collective of five roles, called the Directorate. Governance is based on specialized oversight and collective accountability.

### Core Roles

* **Director of Operations:** Manages scheduling, lab access, and administrative deadlocks.

* **Director of Technology:** Oversees technical infrastructure, safety reviews, and security protocols.

* **Director of Research & Grants:** Stewards external funding, government mandates, and academic integrity.

* **Director of User Experience & Outreach:** Manages the public-facing brand, bootcamps, and community impact.

* **Director of Documentation:** Maintains the SOP library, Archived Repositories, and House Style compliance.

### Decision-Making & Digital Voting

* **The Voting Window:** All proposals are subject to a 48-hour asynchronous window.

* **Governance Decisions:** Changes to this Constitution or the Directorate require a simple majority to pass.

* **Project & Tenure Decisions:** Project proposals and tenure require a Domain Triad. This must include the Director of Technology, the Director of Research & Grants, and at least one other Director.

* **Transparency Levels:** Votes on Governance are public records. Votes and feedback on Project Proposals and Tenure are private and delivered directly to the applicant to protect the lab's Safe-to-Fail culture.

### Deadlocks & Escalation

* **Internal Resolution:** In the event of a tie, the Director of Operations breaks administrative deadlocks, while the Director of Technology breaks technical and safety deadlocks.

* **Institutional Escalation:** If the Directorate reaches an irreconcilable deadlock, or if the lab fails to meet its institutional mandates, the matter is automatically escalated to the CSIT Coordinator for final arbitration.

## Stewardship
The lab's longevity depends on the responsible management of resources and institutional commitments.

### Resource Allocation
To ensure the lab meets its institutional obligations while fostering research and development, resources are allocated according to the following strict precedence:

* **Grant Mandates:** Resources explicitly required for active, externally funded deliverables. These assets are ring-fenced and unavailable for general use during the funding cycle.

* **Active Projects:** Resources allocated to members with an approved project proposal and documented Active Momentum.

* **General Experimentation:** Surplus resources available for short-term prototyping and sandbox testing.

### Grant Deliverables & External Mandates
The Director of Research & Grants acts as the primary liaison between the lab and institutional funders, ensuring all contractual obligations are met without compromising the lab's core research mission.

* **Community-Facing Activities:** The lab executes community-facing activities only when they are explicitly mandated and fully funded by external grants.

* **Fiscal & Resource Neutrality:** Mandated activities must be resource-positive or resource-neutral. Grant funding must cover all consumables, specialized hardware, and administrative overhead. Lab-owned research equipment may not be risked in community-facing activities unless specified in the [Resource Allocation SOP](https://github.com/libertyintelligencelabs/sop-library/blob/main/procedures/resource-allocation.md).

* **Mission Alignment:** Where possible, community-facing activities shall be utilized as Beta-Testing environments for internal lab projects, providing researchers with real-world data and user feedback.

* **Automatic Sunset Clause:** Upon the conclusion of a grant's funding cycle, all associated community-facing obligations are considered fulfilled. The lab will immediately pivot 100% of its operational bandwidth back to internal research and development.

## Compliance & Tenure
Participation is project-based and contingent upon adherence to the lab's standards.

* **Frameworks:** All members must adhere to the [Code of Conduct](https://github.com/libertyintelligencelabs/.github/blob/main/CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md), the [Safety & Ethics Framework](https://github.com/libertyintelligencelabs/.github/blob/main/SAFETY_AND_ETHICS.md), and the [Project Life Cycle SOP](https://github.com/libertyintelligencelabs/sop-library/blob/main/procedures/project-life-cycle.md).

* **Enforcement:** The Directorate holds the authority to terminate tenure for significant stalls in progress or ethical breaches.

* **Security:** Unauthorized use of lab resources for malicious hacking or illegal activities results in immediate, permanent expulsion and a report to the CSIT Department.

## Succession & Maintenance
To ensure the lab's continuity, governance is version-controlled and cyclical.

* **Succession:** Incoming Directors are nominated by the outgoing Directorate and must be ratified by the CSIT Coordinator.

* **House Style:** All outputs must pass the automated linters and documentation standards managed by the Director of Documentation.

* **Amendments:** This Constitution may be amended and updated via Pull Request. A Pull Request must be approved by 4/5 Directors to be merged.

## Sunset
To prevent the persistence of an inactive organization, Liberty Intelligence Labs will enter a state of Automatic Dissolution if any of the following conditions are met:

* **Zero Momentum:** The lab fails to approve or progress a single Active Project for two consecutive semesters.

* **Directorate Collapse:** The Directorate falls below three active members and fails to nominate successors within one semester.

* **Safety Breach:** The CSIT Department revokes backing due to a catastrophic breach of the Safety & Ethics Framework.

Upon sunset, all grant-funded assets return to the funding body, all lab-owned assets are surrendered to the CSIT Department, and the digital repository is archived.
