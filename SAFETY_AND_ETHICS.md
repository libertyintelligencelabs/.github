# Liberty Intelligence Labs: Safety & Ethics Framework
**Liberty Intelligence Labs** is a project-based collective exploring the frontiers of AIoT and cybersecurity through research and experiments that carry inherent digital and physical risks. This Safety & Ethics Framework defines our mandatory boundaries for risk management and legal compliance to ensure all lab activities maintain the physical safety, operational integrity, and institutional responsibility necessary to support our work.

## Core Philosophy
We play in a sandbox so the college doesn't have to.
  
* **No Harm:** Never test exploits or intrusive AI models on college infrastructure, faculty devices, or fellow students without explicit written authorization.

## Hardware & Physical Safety
* **Supervision & Power:** No high-voltage modifications or custom power-supply manipulation may be conducted without the explicit, written clearance of the Director of Technology.

* **Asset Accountability:** All microcontrollers and sensors checked out from the Equipment Ledger remain the property of the lab. Damaged or lost gear must be reported within 24 hours.

## Software & AI Safety
* **Target Isolation:**  All active exploit testing, network scanning, malware analysis, or vulnerability fuzzing must be contained entirely within isolated sandbox environments or designated lab virtual machines. No testing may cross into the active college Wi-Fi, local campus networks, or public internet infrastructure without explicit authorization from the Directorate.

* **Algorithmic Guardrails:** AI models developed or fine-tuned within the lab must not be trained on or used to generate malicious code, automated social-engineering payloads, or deepfakes intended for deception.

* **Resource Guarding:** Computational workloads, such as heavy AI training loops or automated security scripts, must be scheduled and monitored so that they do not degrade lab infrastructure, hog shared workspace bandwidth, or disrupt other members’ projects.

## Responsible Disclosure
If you discover a vulnerability, zero-day, or security flaw in any target, including college infrastructure, local networks, or third-party assets, during your tenure:

* Stop testing immediately. Do not exploit, pivot through, or expand your footprint in the vulnerable system.

* Report the finding exclusively to the **Director of Technology** via secure channels within 12 hours.

* Do not document the flaw in public GitHub issues or share it with peers. Public disclosure is strictly prohibited until the affected organization has patched the flaw and the Directorate provides written authorization.

## Limitation of Liability & Indemnification

* **Individual Accountability:** Liberty Intelligence Labs operates strictly as a modular sandbox for disciplined academic experimentation. Any research, testing, or tool deployment conducted outside the explicitly authorized boundaries of an authorized project is the sole legal and ethical responsibility of the individual.

* **Institutional Exemption:** This Framework indemnifies Liberty Intelligence Labs, its Directorate, and the CSIT Department at Liberty College against any and all liability, claims, disciplinary actions, or legal consequences resulting from a member's noncompliance, unauthorized network activity, or misuse of hardware and software assets.

## Ethical Conduct & Compliance
* **No Malicious Activity:** The use of lab assets, infrastructure, codebases, or network privileges for unauthorized scanning, credential harvesting, or malicious hacking will result in immediate, permanent expulsion.

* **Institutional Escalation:** Serious ethical breaches or legal violations will be automatically escalated to the CSIT Coordinator for disciplinary and legal action.
